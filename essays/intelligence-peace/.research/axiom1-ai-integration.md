## SOURCE 1: MacAskill & Moorhouse, "Preparing for the Intelligence Explosion"

### Full Citation
William MacAskill and Fin Moorhouse, "Preparing for the Intelligence Explosion," Forethought Centre for AI Strategy, March 11, 2025. Available at: https://www.forethought.org/research/preparing-for-the-intelligence-explosion
PDF: https://www.forethought.org/research/preparing-for-the-intelligence-explosion.pdf
arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.14863

### Tier Rating: **Tier 1** (Academic paper by prominent researchers at a dedicated AI strategy institute; widely discussed in EA Forum, LessWrong, 80,000 Hours)

### Key Findings and Data Points

**1. "Century in a Decade" Central Claim:**
Even under conservative assumptions -- no software feedback loop, compute scaling halts in the near term, algorithmic efficiency improvements slow -- the authors find we should still expect "a century's worth of progress in a decade" once AI meaningfully substitutes for human researchers.

**2. Compute Scaling Projections:**
- Training compute can continue to scale for roughly another **10,000x increase** before running into power and other limits (likely well within a decade).
- Assuming the same ratio of compute scale-up to efficiency advances, there will be a further **1,000x increase in algorithmic efficiency** in training.
- Combined: a further **ten-million-fold (10^7) increase in effective training compute** over a decade.

**3. AI Research Capability Multiplier:**
- **Conservative scenario** (compute scaling halts, algorithmic efficiency improvements somewhat slow): **ten-billion-fold (10^10) increase** in AI research capability over ten years.
- **Aggressive scenario** (software feedback loop achieved): **hundred-trillion-fold (10^14) increase** in AI research capability.

**4. The Grand Challenges Framework:**
The paper identifies ten categories of "grand challenges" -- consequential, hard-to-reverse decisions that an intelligence explosion will force:
1. AI takeover
2. Highly destructive technologies (bioweapons, drone swarms, nanotechnology)
3. Power-concentrating mechanisms
4. Value lock-in mechanisms
5. AI agents and digital minds
6. Space governance (offworld resource allocation)
7. New competitive pressures
8. Epistemic disruption
9. Abundance
10. Unknown unknowns

**5. Key Methodological Insight:**
These grand challenges "cannot always be delegated to future AI systems" -- human preparation is required now, before AI reaches transformative capability.

### Relevance to Axiom 1
**Direct and foundational.** The MacAskill-Moorhouse framework provides the strongest quantitative case that AI capability will accelerate at rates that make it the dominant factor in economic production and military capability. The 10^7 to 10^14 multiplier on effective research capability means that AI-augmented economies will operate at fundamentally different scales from non-AI economies. The grand challenges framework -- particularly "highly destructive technologies" and "power-concentrating mechanisms" -- directly supports the claim that AI becomes the substrate of military power.

## SOURCE 5: Drago & Laine, "The Intelligence Curse"

### Full Citation
Luke Drago and Rudolf Laine, "The Intelligence Curse," April 2025. Available at: https://intelligence-curse.ai/
PDF: https://intelligence-curse.ai/intelligence-curse.pdf
Essay series: https://lukedrago.substack.com/p/the-intelligence-curse-an-essay-series
See also: Rudolf Laine, "The Intelligence Curse: An Essay Series," https://www.nosetgauge.com/p/the-intelligence-curse-an-essay-series

**Tier Rating: Tier 2** (Independent researchers; widely discussed in EA/AI safety circles but not peer-reviewed in traditional academic venues)

### Core Thesis
The "intelligence curse" is an analogy to the resource curse: once AI can automate most or all human labor, non-human factors of production (capital, resources, AI control) become overwhelmingly more important than human labor. This severs the historical incentive for powerful actors to invest in their populations.

### Key Arguments and Data Points

**1. The Resource Curse Analogy:**
- States that rely on resource rents rather than taxing human labor suffer from the resource curse -- they do worse at improving citizens' living standards despite natural income sources.
- With AGI, powerful actors will face analogous "rentier state" incentives: revenues come from intelligence rather than people, eliminating returns on investment in education, employment, or welfare.

**2. The Social Contract Breaks:**
- The strong economic incentive that has existed throughout human history -- where powerful actors *need* regular people -- would be severed.
- Under AGI, the relationship between elites and populations shifts from interdependence to reliance on mere goodwill.
- Social mobility stops, ending the social dynamism and progress it drives.

**3. Three-Part Solution Framework:**
- **Avert:** Technical safety measures to prevent catastrophic misuse.
- **Diffuse:** Widely distribute human-augmenting tools, aligning AI to individual users so economic value remains tied to people, not platforms.
- **Democratize:** Ban AI from owning assets or board seats; channel excess rents into citizen wealth funds and participatory audits.

### Key Counterpoints to Techno-Optimism
- The paper directly challenges the assumption that AGI wealth will "trickle down" -- it argues the incentive structure actively works against redistribution.
- Questions whether democratic institutions can survive when elites no longer depend on popular consent for economic production.
- Warns of "gradual or sudden disempowerment of the vast majority of humanity."

### Relevance to Axiom 1
**Indirect but important.** Drago & Laine actually *presuppose* Axiom 1 -- their entire argument depends on AI becoming the substrate of economic power. Their contribution is showing *what follows* from that assumption: not just economic transformation but a fundamental restructuring of the social contract. For the essay, this source is most useful as a secondary citation that takes Axiom 1 as given and explores its political consequences. It also provides a counterpoint framework (the "curse" dimension) that adds nuance to the Intelligence Peace Thesis -- the same axiom that enables peace through mutual deterrence also enables authoritarian concentration of power.

## SOURCE 6: Leopold Aschenbrenner, "Situational Awareness: The Decade Ahead"

### Full Citation
Leopold Aschenbrenner, "Situational Awareness: The Decade Ahead," June 2024. Available at: https://situational-awareness.ai/
PDF: https://situational-awareness.ai/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/situationalawareness.pdf
See also: "Situational Awareness: A One-Year Retrospective," LessWrong, 2025. https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/EGGruXRxGQx6RWT8x/situational-awareness-a-one-year-retrospective

**Tier Rating: Tier 2** (Former OpenAI researcher; influential but self-published, not peer-reviewed; some claims disputed)

### Key Data Points and Arguments

**1. Orders of Magnitude (OOM) Framework:**
- From GPT-2 to GPT-4 (four years): approximately **4-5 OOMs** of effective compute improvement across compute, algorithms, and "unhobbling."
- Effective compute growing at roughly **half an order of magnitude per year** for single training clusters.
- Annual doubling for aggregate investment, chip shipments, and power demand.

**2. The Trillion-Dollar Cluster:**
- The ~2030 training cluster on current trends represents **+4 OOMs** from the GPT-4 cluster.
- Would require approximately **100 GW of power** -- equivalent to more than **20% of total U.S. electricity production**.
- Cost: in the trillion-dollar range.

**3. Intelligence Explosion Projection:**
- Hundreds of millions of AGIs could automate AI research.
- This could **compress a decade of algorithmic progress (5+ OOMs) into one year or less**.

**4. "The Project" -- Government Nationalization:**
- Predicts the U.S. government will create a government AGI project by **2027/28**.
- Leading AI labs will be forced to merge; Congress will appropriate trillions for compute clusters; core AGI teams will move to secure facilities.
- Argument: "It's an insane proposition that the US government will let a random SF startup develop superintelligence."

**5. Geopolitical Stakes:**
- "Those who have superintelligence will wield complete dominance over those who don't."
- A few years' lead could provide "as decisive a military advantage as the US coalition had against Iraq in the Gulf War."
- China reaching superintelligence first "could enable a stable totalitarian world regime."

**6. Security Vulnerability:**
- Current AI labs "treat security as an afterthought" and are "essentially handing AGI secrets to the Chinese Communist Party."
- "The leading Chinese AGI labs won't be in Beijing or Shanghai -- they'll be in San Francisco and London."

### Critiques and Reactions
- **Scott Aaronson** (UT Austin/OpenAI): Called it "one of the most extraordinary documents I've ever read."
- **Critics** on EA Forum: Argue Aschenbrenner is "drastically mischaracterizing the situation" and that his narrative "undermines safety and threatens humanity" by implicitly encouraging a race dynamic.
- **Benchmark criticism:** Using benchmarks meant to measure human performance to gauge LLMs "might be extremely misleading."
- **Real-world impact:** Aschenbrenner used "Situational Awareness" to launch a **$1.5 billion hedge fund** backed by the Collison brothers (Stripe).

### Relevance to Axiom 1
**Directly supportive, with caveats.** Aschenbrenner's framework is perhaps the most aggressive version of Axiom 1 -- he argues AI will become not merely the substrate but the *decisive factor* in national power, comparable to nuclear weapons. His OOM framework provides a concrete, quantifiable mechanism for how this happens. The "trillion-dollar cluster" concept illustrates the scale of infrastructure investment that states will undertake, reinforcing the idea that AI becomes embedded in national economic and military architecture. However, his predictions are among the most extreme in the field and should be cited as representing the aggressive end of credible forecasts.

## SOURCE 7: Open-Source AI vs. Hardware Dependency

### Full Citations
- RAND Corporation, "The Rise of DeepSeek: What the Headlines Miss," January 2025. https://www.rand.org/pubs/commentary/2025/01/the-rise-of-deepseek-what-the-headlines-miss.html
- RAND Corporation, "Leashing Chinese AI Needs Smart Chip Controls," August 2025. https://www.rand.org/pubs/commentary/2025/08/leashing-chinese-ai-needs-smart-chip-controls.html
- CSIS, "DeepSeek, Huawei, Export Controls, and the Future of the U.S.-China AI Race." https://www.csis.org/analysis/deepseek-huawei-export-controls-and-future-us-china-ai-race
- Tom's Hardware, "DeepSeek's AI Breakthrough Bypasses Industry-Standard CUDA." https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/deepseeks-ai-breakthrough-bypasses-industry-standard-cuda-uses-assembly-like-ptx-programming-instead
- Deloitte, "2026 Semiconductor Industry Outlook." https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/technology-media-telecom-outlooks/semiconductor-industry-outlook.html

**Tier Rating: Tier 1** (RAND, CSIS, Deloitte -- established policy and industry research institutions)

### Key Findings

**1. DeepSeek: Efficiency Gains, but Hardware Dependency Persists:**
- DeepSeek-V3 trained on **2,048 NVIDIA H800 GPUs** (sanctions-compliant version of H100 with interconnect bandwidth throttled from 900 GB/s to ~400 GB/s).
- Reported training cost: **$5.576 million** (only for official training run; excludes prior R&D and ablation experiments).
- DeepSeek also has access to **10,000 A100 GPUs** per published papers.
- True cost estimated by critics at up to **$1.6 billion** when including all R&D, hardware acquisition, and prior experimentation.
- DeepSeek overcame hardware limitations through algorithmic innovation (custom inter-chip communication, dropping to PTX assembly-like programming for 20 of 132 processing units on each H800).

**2. The Hardware Chokepoint:**
- Despite algorithmic innovation, Chinese AI developers **overwhelmingly prefer using NVIDIA chips** -- even severely performance-degraded ones.
- In anticipation of the U.S. ban on NVIDIA's H20 chips, ByteDance, Alibaba, and Tencent **rushed to spend $16 billion to stockpile 1.3-1.6 million H20 units**.
- The U.S. currently has a **tenfold advantage over China in total compute capacity**.
- China's AI achievements have "one glaring weak spot: access to compute."

**3. Meta's Llama -- Scale of Hardware Requirements:**
- Llama 3.1 405B trained on **over 16,000 H100 GPUs** across two custom-built 24K GPU clusters.
- Total training: **39.3 million GPU hours** (30.84M for the 405B model alone).
- Over **15 trillion tokens** of training data.
- Despite being "open-source," running the full 405B model requires an H100 node (8x H100, ~640GB VRAM) or multi-node setup.

**4. NVIDIA and TSMC Market Dominance:**
- NVIDIA holds **80% market share** of the global GPU semiconductor chip market.
- NVIDIA expected to report **$49 billion in AI-related revenue in 2025** (up 39% YoY).
- TSMC reached **72% market share** in 2025.
- TSMC dedicates **over 28% of total wafer capacity** to AI chip manufacturing.
- TSMC manufactures **80-90% of sub-7nm chips**, mainly in Taiwan.
- Three American companies account for **over 75% of advanced chip design**.
- Global semiconductor industry expected to reach **$975 billion in annual sales in 2026**.

**5. CHIPS Act and Supply Chain Strategy:**
- TSMC received **$6.6 billion** in CHIPS Act funding for Arizona fabrication plants.
- TSMC investing **over $100 billion** in U.S. manufacturing long-term.
- Arizona plants will produce advanced 3nm and 4nm chips for Apple, NVIDIA, and other customers.

### Relevance to Axiom 1
**Critical supporting evidence.** This section establishes that even "open-source" AI remains fundamentally dependent on a hardware supply chain that is concentrated in U.S.-allied hands (NVIDIA design, TSMC manufacturing). The DeepSeek case -- often cited as evidence that software can overcome hardware limitations -- actually reinforces the axiom: DeepSeek still required thousands of NVIDIA GPUs, and China's response to export controls was to stockpile $16B in chips, demonstrating that hardware access is treated as a strategic resource. The 80% NVIDIA GPU market share and 80-90% TSMC sub-7nm manufacturing concentration mean that the AI substrate has a geographic and geopolitical bottleneck that makes it inherently a matter of national power.

### Recommended Framing for the Essay

For Foreign Affairs readers, Axiom 1 can be stated with high confidence based on three convergent arguments:

1. **The economic case is already empirical, not speculative.** AI investment is exceeding dot-com boom levels (St. Louis Fed), 78% of organizations are adopting AI (McKinsey), and the world's leading economic forecasters project $7-23 trillion in annual impact. AI is not *becoming* the substrate of economic power -- it is *already becoming* it.

2. **The military case is revealed preference.** Both the United States ($13.4B FY2026 autonomy budget) and China (343 PLA AI contracts, DeepSeek military integration) are investing as if AI dominance equals military dominance. The Pentagon's own AI strategy treats AI as foundational infrastructure, not an add-on capability.

3. **The hardware chokepoint makes it geopolitical.** Because the AI supply chain funnels through NVIDIA (80% GPU share) and TSMC (80-90% sub-7nm manufacturing), control over AI infrastructure is inherently a matter of national security. China's $16B chip stockpiling and the CHIPS Act both confirm that governments treat AI hardware as a strategic resource on par with oil or nuclear materials.

### Gaps and Caveats for the Essay

1. **The adoption-impact gap:** While adoption rates are high, only 39% of companies report enterprise-level EBIT impact (McKinsey). Goldman Sachs notes current measured GDP impact is only 0.2-0.4%. The transformation is real but the full economic substrate effect is still ahead, not behind us.

2. **AGI timeline uncertainty:** The interquartile range on Metaculus (2027-2039) is extremely wide. The essay should acknowledge that Axiom 1 holds even under longer timelines -- narrow AI is already becoming the economic and military substrate, and AGI would simply accelerate the process.

3. **The Drago-Laine counterpoint:** The same axiom that enables "intelligence peace" also enables the "intelligence curse." If AI becomes the substrate of power, those who control it may have diminished incentives to maintain the social contract. The essay should address this tension directly.

4. **Open-source complication:** DeepSeek demonstrates that algorithmic innovation can partially offset hardware disadvantage. While the hardware chokepoint remains real, the essay should not overstate the permanence of current supply chain concentration.
