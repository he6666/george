# Seeds: The Intelligence Peace Thesis

> Compiled from author's hunch (00-hunch.md) and working session notes (.research/session-notes.md).

## 1. Foundational Sources

- **Chris Miller, *Chip War* (2022)** — The definitive account of semiconductor supply chain structure. Starting point for Axioms 2–3.
- **Dale Copeland, *Economic Interdependence and War* (Princeton, 2015)** — Trade expectations theory. Winner of 2017 ISA Best Book Award. MUST engage head-on: negative expectations about future access trigger preventive war.
- **Henry Farrell & Abraham Newman, "Weaponized Interdependence," *International Security* 44:1 (2019)** — Chokepoint power framework. Shows network hubs confer real, exercisable coercive power.
- **Farrell & Newman, "The Weaponized World Economy," *Foreign Affairs* (Sep/Oct 2025)** — Updated argument. US should "turn away from unilateralism, toward détente and arms control."
- **Leopold Aschenbrenner, "Situational Awareness: The Decade Ahead" (2024)** — AGI by 2027 thesis, intelligence explosion, industrial mobilization.
- **MacAskill & Moorhouse, "Preparing for the Intelligence Explosion" (Forethought, 2025)** — Grand challenges framework. Century of progress in a decade. Directly relevant to Axiom 1.

## 2. Key Thinkers

- **Dale Copeland** — Trade expectations theory (the strongest theoretical challenge)
- **Henry Farrell & Abraham Newman** — Weaponized interdependence (the most sophisticated framework to engage)
- **Chris Miller** — Semiconductor supply chain empirics
- **Oneal & Russett** — Commercial peace literature (liberal side)
- **Gartzke & Lupu** — Defense of commercial peace against WWI counterexample
- **Nick Bostrom** — Methodological model (axiomatic deductive style)
- **Mearsheimer** — Structural realism; FA essay style model ("Back to the Future")

## 3. Known Debates

- **Does economic interdependence prevent war?** — Liberals (Oneal & Russett, Gartzke) say yes; realists (Copeland, historical counterexamples) say it depends on expectations about future access.
- **Is the Silicon Shield real?** — Proponents (tech industry, some strategists) vs. skeptics (Defense Priorities: "Semiconductors Are Not a Reason to Defend Taiwan").
- **Weaponized interdependence — offensive or defensive?** — Farrell & Newman show it's offensive (sanctions, export controls). IPT argues the *same structure* generates defensive collective security in the post-AGI world.
- **Can China achieve AI autarky?** — Optimists point to SMIC progress and DeepSeek; realists note SMIC is 5–7 years behind TSMC, China's EUV is 10–15 years behind ASML.

## 4. Author's Position

The thesis (IPT) is genuinely novel — it generalizes the Silicon Shield into a universal mechanism and provides a deductive framework. It sits between liberal institutionalism (interdependence promotes peace) and structural realism (the structure of the system determines outcomes). The novel contribution is showing that *chokepoint* interdependence is categorically different from general trade interdependence.

The counter-intuitive policy conclusion — keep adversaries integrated rather than exclude them — cuts against both US China hawks and the author's own initial instinct toward a "democracy-only club."

## 5. Anti-Sources (to engage/rebut)

- **Copeland** — The strongest theoretical challenge. Must show why AI supply chain dependency is different from the cases where trade expectations theory predicts war.
- **Defense Priorities, "Semiconductors Are Not a Reason to Defend Taiwan"** — The strongest policy critique of the Silicon Shield.
- **Pre-WWI interdependence counterexample** — Must address directly.
- **Drago & Laine, "The Intelligence Curse" (2025)** — Resource curse analogy for AI. Relevant counterpoint.

## 6. Quality Floor

**Foreign Affairs standard:** Peer-reviewed research, major think tank reports (Brookings, RAND, CSIS, CFR, Stimson), government reports, and recognized expert analysis. Smart blog posts (Substack, personal sites) acceptable for framing but not as primary evidence for factual claims.
