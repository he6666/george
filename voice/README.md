# Voice Samples

Place 2-3 of your best essays here as markdown files. The drafting and polish stages reference these to match your natural writing style.

## What the system analyzes

- **Sentence rhythm**: Average sentence length, long/short alternation, use of fragments
- **Formality level**: How academic vs. conversational your default register is
- **Use of analogy and metaphor**: Do you reach for comparisons? Abstract or concrete?
- **Hedging patterns**: How you handle uncertainty — do you hedge a lot or state boldly and correct later?
- **Argumentation style**: Do you build incrementally? Lead with the thesis? Use Socratic questioning? Reason by analogy?
- **Characteristic phrases**: Recurring constructions, sentence structures, or verbal tics
- **Evidence style**: Do you integrate evidence in-line or set it apart? Do you quote directly or paraphrase?
- **Use of first person**: How much you put yourself in the essay
- **Paragraph structure**: Short punchy paragraphs or long developed ones?
- **How you open and close**: Your default hooks and endings

## How to choose samples

Pick essays that represent **how you want to sound**, not necessarily your most polished work. The system will try to match the voice in these samples, so choose writing where your natural style comes through.

Ideally:
- 1 essay close to the target publication's style (if writing for Foreign Affairs, include a policy-focused piece)
- 1 essay in your most natural/comfortable voice
- 1 essay that shows how you handle complex arguments or evidence

## File format

Save essays as markdown files in this directory:
```
voice/
  my-best-essay.md
  another-piece.md
  policy-analysis.md
```

If you don't have essays to include, the system will still work — it just won't have a voice reference and will default to matching the publication's style guide.
